# Balance Guide

**Status:** Stable baseline at v0.0.0. Material changes normally require an ADR.

Balance means interesting alternatives within RimWorld's economy, threats, labor,
space, time, technology, and storytelling systems. It does not mean identical
numbers.

## Required Comparison

Every feature identifies its closest vanilla or DLC alternatives and compares:

- acquisition, research, skill, and infrastructure requirements
- material, labor, power, space, upkeep, and opportunity costs
- output, reliability, flexibility, timing, and scaling
- failure modes, counters, environmental constraints, and automation
- early-, mid-, and late-game value
- colony wealth, raid pressure, mood, ideology, and storyteller implications

## Tradeoff Rule

A strength must create or intensify a decision. Valid tradeoffs include climate
or terrain dependence, slower throughput, specialist labor, storage burden,
volatility, social constraints, delayed payoff, maintenance, scarcity, or a
different risk profile. Cosmetic novelty is not a balancing cost.

## Balance Process

1. State the intended player decision and balance hypothesis.
2. Establish a vanilla baseline from current game data.
3. Model best, typical, and poor conditions.
4. Define abuse cases and stacking interactions.
5. Set observable playtest questions, not just target numbers.
6. Test at relevant colony stages and difficulty contexts.
7. Record changes and why the evidence changed the hypothesis.

Avoid percentage budgets treated as universal truth. RimWorld value is systemic;
use calculations to expose assumptions, then validate through play.
