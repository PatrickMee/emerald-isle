## The Mod We Are Building

Emerald Isle is a focused, lore-friendly RimWorld expansion about building and
living in a culture shaped by Irish historical, archaeological, linguistic, and
mythological inspiration. It is not a recreation of Ireland. It asks how material
constraints, practices, stories, and social ideas associated with Ireland could be
transformed into a distinct culture on the Rim.

Emerald Isle is not an early-medieval technology tier. Early medieval sources and
ordinary domestic life provide the initial roots, but the product promise is a
culture that adapts as a colony moves from first holding to mature settlement and
later RimWorld pressures.

The colony remains the center of play. Emerald Isle should deepen ordinary RimWorld
decisions—what to grow, build, preserve, value, risk, remember, and pass on—without
requiring the player to learn a separate game. The result should be recognizable to
players familiar with Ireland and immediately legible to players who are not.

## What Makes Emerald Isle Distinct

Emerald Isle expresses identity through connected material and social relationships,
not a collection of themed objects. Crops matter because they shape labor and food.
Buildings matter because materials, defense, climate, and settlement form shape
colony life. Language, art, ritual, and stories matter because they give those systems
meaning. No single green palette, knot pattern, translated word, creature, or famous
monument can carry the identity by itself.

The mod's distinctive promise is disciplined transformation:

- archaeological and historical evidence defines a credible design space;
- cultural context prevents generic “Celtic” shorthand and romantic reconstruction;
- Irish-inspired identity persists through adaptation rather than remaining fixed to
  one historical technology phase;
- RimWorld's systems turn that material into choices, costs, failures, and stories;
- restrained presentation makes the result feel native to the base game; and
- small, interoperable features accumulate into a coherent way of life.

An Emerald Isle feature should still be interesting if its cultural label is hidden,
and its Irish inspiration should still materially shape the final design when the
label is restored.

## What Finished Looks Like

Version 1.0 is finished when Emerald Isle feels like one coherent expansion rather
than an assortment of additions. A player can establish, sustain, and recognize an
Irish-inspired holding through normal colony play. Its content creates several
viable strategies, produces memorable setbacks and successes, and remains useful
alongside vanilla content rather than replacing it.

The culture should remain recognizable as the colony matures. Early objects may be
superseded, upgraded, or made situational by later play, but the colony should not
stop feeling like Emerald Isle once vanilla progression reaches industry, high
wealth, advanced research, or DLC systems.

Finished does not mean exhaustive coverage of Irish history or culture. It means the
core promise is complete, understandable, balanced, maintainable, and trustworthy.
The supported experience loads cleanly, survives long colonies and saves, respects
its compatibility commitments, performs within project budgets, and explains itself
through RimWorld's normal interface. Art reads beside vanilla art. Names and writing
are functional and culturally responsible. A new contributor can trace every major
claim and decision through the repository.

The measure is quality and cohesion, not item count. A smaller set of systems that
interact well is more complete than a larger catalogue whose pieces do not affect one
another.

## Principles for Future Features

Future features should follow these rules:

1. **Start with a player decision.** Historical presence alone does not justify a
   feature. It must change planning, risk, labor, relationships, or story.
2. **Transform rather than reproduce.** Preserve the meaningful constraints and
   relationships in the source material while rewriting literal Earth history for
   RimWorld's setting.
3. **Preserve cultural continuity through adaptation.** Irish inspiration is a
   living design language, not a fixed historical tech tier. Later-game features
   should evolve recognizable roots instead of abandoning them or reskinning
   generic technology.
4. **Prefer ordinary life before spectacle.** Food, work, shelter, craft, exchange,
   memory, and community establish identity more reliably than monuments or famous
   myths.
5. **Create situational strengths.** Every advantage must have an observable cost or
   opportunity cost. Emerald Isle content must not become the automatic best choice.
6. **Use RimWorld's language.** Prefer familiar jobs, bills, stats, information,
   threats, and feedback. New systems must earn their conceptual and maintenance cost.
7. **Ship the smallest coherent form.** Build only what the current feature needs.
   Preserve stable extension seams without constructing speculative frameworks.
8. **Remain understandable without prior knowledge.** Research should deepen the
   feature, not become homework for the player.
9. **Treat compatibility as product quality.** Optional DLC and mod interactions
   fail safely; public identifiers and save-visible behavior are durable promises.
10. **Let evidence narrow scope.** Research, testing, and playtesting may simplify or
    reject a feature when its gameplay value does not justify its burden.

## Balancing Evidence and Play

Archaeology and history establish what is supported, uncertain, regional,
anachronistic, or sensitive. They supply relationships worth preserving: materials
and methods, labor and status, landscape and settlement, food and season, belief and
memory. The project records uncertainty instead of filling gaps with confident
invention.

Gameplay then determines whether and how that evidence enters the mod. Emerald Isle
does not simulate labor merely because people performed it, reproduce hierarchy
because a text records it, or add an object because it survives in a collection. A
historically strong idea may be rejected if it adds only clicks. A simplified design
may be preferred when it preserves the source relationship while producing clearer
choices and better stories.

RimWorld's design philosophy is the final medium. Features should participate in its
interlocking economy of time, pawn work, space, danger, mood, wealth, environment,
and recovery. Balance is judged across the whole lifecycle and in play, not by one
statistic. Historical credibility constrains the fiction; cultural care constrains
the framing; gameplay justifies inclusion; and vanilla fit determines how the player
experiences it.

Emerald Isle is complete when those four obligations reinforce one another without
any one becoming an excuse to neglect the others.
