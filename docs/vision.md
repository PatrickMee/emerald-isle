# Product Vision

## Promise

Emerald Isle lets players build and encounter a distinctly Irish-inspired Rim
culture through systems that generate hard choices, social texture, and colony
stories. Recognition should be immediate, but the result must feel native to
RimWorld rather than imported from Earth.

## Design Pillars

1. **Recognizable, transformed inspiration**: forms and ideas evoke Ireland while
   names, fiction, and causality belong to the Rim.
2. **Decisions over decoration**: content earns inclusion by changing how the
   player plans, risks, adapts, or tells stories.
3. **Advantages with consequences**: strengths create new vulnerabilities,
   costs, dependencies, or timing problems.
4. **Systemic storytelling**: interacting systems produce memorable outcomes
   rather than scripted spectacle alone.
5. **Vanilla-quality restraint**: concise UI, readable silhouettes, coherent
   mechanics, and minimal conceptual overhead.
6. **Respectful plurality**: avoid reducing a varied living culture to stereotypes,
   tourist symbols, or a single historical period.

## Audience

- RimWorld players who value lore-friendly content and emergent stories
- Players interested in culturally grounded themes without simulationist burden
- Modders seeking a compatible platform for optional future integrations

No prior knowledge of Irish history or language may be required to understand a
mechanic.

## Boundaries

Emerald Isle is not Earth, a historical simulator, a nationalist narrative, a
fantasy overhaul, or a collection of unconnected Irish-themed objects. It does
not use authenticity to excuse weak gameplay and does not use mythology as a
license for generic fantasy.

## Product Test

A proposed feature belongs when all answers are yes:

- Would it still be interesting if its Irish inspiration were hidden?
- Does the inspiration materially shape the mechanic or presentation?
- Can a player understand its purpose through RimWorld's normal language?
- Does it create a choice rather than a direct upgrade?
- Can it ship as a bounded slice without requiring the entire long-term vision?

## Milestone 0 Exit

The studio foundation is complete when contributors can take a feature from a
research question to a release decision using documented roles, gates, templates,
technical boundaries, and evidence standards without inventing process ad hoc.
