## Summary

The First Holding is Emerald Isle's first playable preview. It adds a small,
cohesive Irish-inspired colony slice using RimWorld's vanilla agriculture,
construction, bill, crafting, cooking, storage, trade, and food systems.

## Included Content

- Oats: a medium-cycle grain crop with ordinary food, feed, storage, trade, and
  caravan uses.
- Dry-stone wall: a stone-only linked wall that uses fewer blocks than a vanilla
  stone wall in exchange for lower durability and greater construction work.
- Hand quern: a compact, unpowered stone worktable for milling oats.
- Milled oats: a prepared cooking ingredient produced from raw oats.
- Oat porridge: a fresh simple-tier food with low final cooking work and a short
  shelf life.
- Oat flatbread: a labor-intensive simple-tier food with moderate keeping time.

The release is XML-only. It contains no C#, Harmony patches, required DLC,
custom framework, powered mill, wall variants, or new stone resources.

## Installation

1. Copy the staged `EmeraldIsle` folder into RimWorld's local `Mods` directory.
2. Confirm the resulting path contains `EmeraldIsle/About/About.xml` rather than
   an additional nested `EmeraldIsle` folder.
3. Enable **Emerald Isle** after **Core** in the RimWorld Mods list and restart
   when prompted.

The developer-testing package is separate and must not be installed for normal
play or included in a public release archive.

## Upgrade and Existing Saves

Version 0.1.0 is the first gameplay release; `v0.0.0` contained project
foundation documents only. Adding Emerald Isle to an existing RimWorld 1.6 save
is expected to make its crops, construction, and production content available
without migration or custom serialized state.

Back up important saves before changing any mod list. Removing Emerald Isle from
a save that contains its plants, items, buildings, blueprints, bills, food-policy
references, stockpile filters, inventories, or caravan contents can produce
ordinary missing-definition warnings and remove affected content. Consume or
remove mod content first where practical, save a backup, and expect content loss.

## Compatibility and Intended Behavior

- Core-only operation is required; supported DLC add no Version 0.1-specific
  behavior.
- Raw oats remain edible plant food and may be disliked when eaten uncooked.
- Milling uses Crafting labor; final oat-food preparation uses Cooking labor.
- The hand quern is intentionally unpowered. Powered milling is outside Version
  0.1 scope.
- Oat porridge and oat flatbread are simple-tier everyday foods, not luxury or
  preserved survival meals.
