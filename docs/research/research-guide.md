# Historical and Cultural Research Guide

Research exists to generate responsible, specific design possibilities. It is
not a search for decorative facts or a claim that one source defines Ireland.

## Source Practice

Prefer peer-reviewed scholarship, museums, archives, heritage bodies, primary
sources with context, specialist dictionaries, and recognized language bodies.
Use popular summaries for discovery, not as the sole support for disputed claims.
Record author, title, publisher, date, URL or identifier, access date, relevant
pages, source type, and license for reusable media.

Triangulate important claims across independent sources. Distinguish evidence,
scholarly interpretation, folklore, modern revival, and project invention.

## Required Research Brief

Define the question, period, region, communities, terminology, evidence,
uncertainties, contested interpretations, sensitivities, common misconceptions,
gameplay opportunities, transformations required for the Rim, and claims the
feature must not make.

## Cultural Review

Review asks whether the work collapses periods or regions, presents stereotype as
essence, confuses mythology with practiced belief, trivializes conflict or sacred
material, or uses Irish language incorrectly. Irish-language naming should be
reviewed by a competent speaker before release when it is player-facing.

## Transformation Test

The final design must document what came from research, what was altered for
gameplay, what is original Rim fiction, and why the result remains recognizable
without claiming historical reconstruction.
